# Backtest answer key — NeurIPS 2025 (San Diego + Mexico City, Dec 2–7 2025)
_Gathered independently of any forecast/indicator. HIGH conf on awards/stats/workshops; MED-HIGH on theme ranking._

## Story in 3 sentences
The post-o1/R1 REASONING conference: record ~21.6K submissions / ~26K attendees converged on reasoning models, RL-for-LLMs, multimodality, and benchmark-driven evaluation, with diffusion now a third pillar alongside LLMs and RL. The mood turned REFLECTIVE/SKEPTICAL not triumphalist — marquee awards/keynotes probed LIMITS and MECHANISMS (RLVR-skeptic runner-up; "why diffusion doesn't memorize"; "Artificial Hivemind" diversity-collapse Best Paper; Sutton "AI has lost its way"; Choi "jagged intelligence"). Structural milestones: first Mexico City satellite, inaugural Position Paper Track, major anti-collusion review reform, Test-of-Time to Faster R-CNN (memorial to Jian Sun).

## Awards (VERIFIED, NeurIPS blog 2025-11-26)
- **Best Papers (4):** "Artificial Hivemind: The Open-Ended Homogeneity of LMs" (mode collapse/diversity, Infinity-Chat dataset); "Gated Attention for LLMs: Non-linearity, Sparsity, Attention-Sink-Free" (Alibaba Qwen, architecture); "1000 Layer Networks for Self-Supervised RL" (scaling RL depth to 1024 layers); "Why Diffusion Models Don't Memorize" (D&B, generalization vs memorization).
- **Runners-up (3):** "Does RL Really Incentivize Reasoning Capacity in LLMs Beyond the Base Model?" (the RLVR-SKEPTIC headline paper); "Optimal Mistake Bounds for Transductive Online Learning" (30-yr-old theory open problem); "Superposition Yields Robust Neural Scaling" (scaling-laws mechanism).
- **Test of Time:** Faster R-CNN (NeurIPS 2015). **Sejnowski–Hinton Prize:** feedback alignment (Lillicrap 2016).
