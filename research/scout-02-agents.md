# Scout 02 — LLM Agents & Agentic Systems
_Filed 2026-06-21. Thesis: frontier pivoted from "can we prompt an agent" to (a) how to TRAIN agents end-to-end (RL+environments), (b) how to MEASURE honestly (eval crisis), (c) how reliable over long horizons (compounding error). Naive 2024/25 themes (multi-agent debate, "more agents=better", static benchmarks) are being actively DEMOLISHED in 2026 lit._

## Emerging concepts
1. **RL Environments & Verifiers as the scaling bottleneck (+ auto env synthesis)**. Field inverted: RL algo (GRPO/GSPO) commoditized; scarce asset = environment + reward checker. Evidence: Prime Intellect `verifiers` lib (GitHub 4.2k★) + Environments Hub; **INTELLECT-3** (2512.16144, 106B MoE trained on open env stack); Endless Terminals (2601.16443, 10.7%→53.3% via 3,255 auto-gen tasks); Agent World Model (2602.10090); Epoch AI: Anthropic >$1B/yr on RL envs; anchor survey "Landscape of Agentic RL for LLMs" (2509.02547, **TMLR v5 Apr 2026, 500+ works**, LLM as POMDP not single-step MDP). **SURGING.** Freshness HIGH ("the environment is the product").
2. **Agent Evaluation Crisis / "Evals-as-Science"**. "Benchmarks are broken" → rigorous program. **BenchJack** (Berkeley RDI/Dawn Song, 2605.12673) — 219 flaws across SWE-bench/WebArena/OSWorld/GAIA/Terminal-Bench; 10-line conftest.py "resolves" all SWE-bench Verified. **BenchGuard** (2604.24955). **DISPOSITIVE: NeurIPS 2026 renamed "Datasets & Benchmarks" → "Evaluations & Datasets (ED)" track** (blog.neurips.cc 2026-03-23), explicitly solicits auditing/stress-testing/negative-results, "Submissions need not outperform prior work." **SURGING.** Freshness VERY HIGH.
3. **Agent Reliability & Task-Horizon Measurement (compounding error)**. Unit shifts from avg accuracy → distributional reliability over long horizons; per-step errors POSITIVELY correlated → super-linear decay. "Towards a Science of AI Agent Reliability" (2602.16666, **ICML 2026**, Kapoor/Narayanan Princeton); **HAL** Holistic Agent Leaderboard (2510.11977, **ICLR 2026**); Beyond pass@1 (2603.29231, 23,392 episodes); **METR Time Horizon 1.1** (Jan 2026, ~320 min for Claude Opus 4.5, doubling ACCELERATING to ~3mo); HORIZON (2604.11978). **SURGING.** Freshness HIGH ("time horizon" as metric is 2026-native).
4. **SWE-bench's successors (post-"Verified" coding-agent eval)**. SWE-bench Verified = saturated/contaminated; **OpenAI Frontier Evals stopped reporting Verified (~Feb 2026)**. **SWE-bench Pro** (2509.16941, Scale AI, 1,865 enterprise tasks, frontier ~40s% vs 70%+ Verified); **Terminal-Bench** (2601.11868, **ICLR 2026**, scores agent+harness pair); contamination cluster at NeurIPS 2025 D&B: SWE-rebench (2505.20411), SWE-bench-Live (2505.23419), SWE-smith (**Spotlight**); "Agent Ability or Model Memory?" (2512.10218, ~3× higher on Verified); **FeatureBench** (2602.10975, ICLR 2026; 74.4% SWE-bench → 11.0% feature-dev). **SURGING.** Freshness HIGH.
5. **Open-source deep-research training stack + "interaction scaling"**. X-R1 search wave → full pipeline (CPT→SFT→RL), Chinese OSS lineages. **Tongyi DeepResearch** (2510.24701, ~19.5k★) + AgentFounder/"Scaling Agents via Continual Pre-training" (2509.13310, **ICLR 2026**); **MiroThinker** (2511.11793, 8.3k★, "interactive scaling" up to 600 tool calls/task). 3 surveys in 6mo. **SURGING.** Freshness HIGH.
6. **Native GUI/Computer-Use foundation agents w/ multi-turn RL**. OSWorld (NeurIPS 2024 anchor) BEATEN past 72.36% human baseline → train dedicated GUI foundation models. GUI-Owl-1.5/Mobile-Agent-v3.5 (2602.16855, 2B–235B); CUA-Suite (2603.24440, ServiceNow, 10K video demos); MobileWorld (2512.19432, ACL 2026); survey 2604.27955. **SURGING (highest GUI volume).** Freshness HIGH (crossing human line is 2026 event).
7. **RL-learned & test-time/parametric agent memory**. Past "RAG over chat history" → learn memory policy via RL + memory as weight updates at test time. Memory-R1 (2508.19828, ADD/UPDATE/DELETE via GRPO); Mem-α (2509.25911); NVIDIA TTT-E2E (Jan 2026); TMEM (2606.04536); **Evo-Memory** (2511.20857, DeepMind — Ed Chi, Fernando Pereira); **MemoryArena** (2602.16313 — long-context-only drops 80%→45%, settles that long context ≠ memory); **ICLR 2026 MemAgents workshop**; Mem0 (~48k★, $24M A), Letta ($10M seed). **SURGING.** Freshness HIGH.

> **DEMOTED (skeptical reckoning): Multi-agent debate / "more agents=better" is being REFUTED, not promoted.** "Single-Agent > Multi-Agent Under Equal Token Budgets" (Kiela, 2604.02460, grounded in Data Processing Inequality); "Why Do Multi-Agent LLM Systems Fail?"/MAST (Berkeley Stoica/Zaharia/Gonzalez, 2503.13657, ICLR 2026, 14 failure modes). Constructive survivor = **joint multi-agent RL co-training** (MARTI ICLR 2026; MAGRPO as Dec-POMDP). Protocols (MCP/A2A) = enterprise/security story, safety workshops only. Verdict: multi-agent prominent at NeurIPS 2026 as CRITIQUE + CO-TRAINING, not debate.

## NeurIPS 2026 prediction candidates
- **P1 Agent Evaluation Crisis = dominant meta-theme; benchmark-auditing & "evals-as-science" prominent (multiple ED-track accepts, award-adjacent).** Novel (inverts "new benchmark beats baselines" → "prove your benchmark is gameable"; negative results first-class). High-prob: NEAR-DISPOSITIVE — NeurIPS RENAMED the track + invites auditing/critique/negative-results; BenchJack/BenchGuard demonstrate method; HAL (ICLR2026)+Reliability (ICML2026) already peer-reviewed. **Conf 90% (highest).** Criterion: ED track ≥5 accepts whose primary contribution is auditing/critiquing agent benchmarks or formalizing agent-eval reliability (vs new SOTA) + ≥1 spotlight/oral/award.
- **P2 RL training of agents inside environments (agentic RL + env/verifier scaling) = headline track, distinct from RLHF.** Novel (shift to "what environment+verifier, what scale, what async infra"; auto-gen envs; "environment is scarce asset"). High-prob: TMLR survey 500+ works; INTELLECT-3; 4.2k★ tooling; >$1B/yr spend; AgentGym-RL was **ICLR 2026 Oral**. **Conf 85%.** Criterion: ≥1 oral/spotlight + dedicated workshop on agentic RL/RL-environments + ≥10 main-track papers training agents end-to-end w/ RL in interactive envs.
- **P3 Long-horizon agent reliability & compounding-error/task-horizon measurement = named thread.** Novel (distributional reliability, quantified horizon-decay, positively-correlated errors; "time horizon" metric 2026-native). High-prob: Princeton (ICML2026), HAL (ICLR2026), METR series, replications. **Conf 75%.** Criterion: ≥3 accepts measuring success-decay-with-horizon / compounding error / operational reliability as central contribution.
- **P4 Post-SWE-bench coding-agent benchmarks (harder/long-horizon/contamination-resistant + terminal-agent eval) prominent in ED track.** Novel (repo-scale, feature-dev not bug-fixing, agent+harness scoring; 74%→11% gaps). High-prob: SWE-rebench/SWE-bench-Live (NeurIPS2025 D&B), SWE-smith (Spotlight); SWE-bench Pro + Terminal-Bench + FeatureBench at ICLR2026. **Conf 80%.** Criterion: ≥3 coding-agent benchmark papers targeting harder-than-Verified + SWE-bench Pro/Terminal-Bench among most-cited.
- **P5 (higher-variance) Multi-agent SKEPTICAL turn + joint multi-agent RL co-training, NOT debate.** Novel (reversal of "collaboration framework beats baseline"). High-prob for reframed version (Kiela; Berkeley MAST at ICLR2026; "Science of Collective AI" position paper). **Conf 65%** (risk is direction not presence). Criterion: prominent multi-agent papers that critically characterize when multi-agent helps/hurts OR jointly RL-train outnumber+outrank naive debate/collaboration frameworks.
